Thesis: Human-Robot Collaboration in Assembly Tasks

Summary: This research investigates how teleoperated robotic systems influence human performance and communication during physical collaboration. By comparing two-person human teams to human-robot teams on a wire assembly task, we evaluated differences in task completion time, workload (NASA-TLX), and communication patterns using Bales IPA.

Aim: To evaluate the impact of robotic assistance on team task performance, workload, and communication dynamics.

Methods: Data collection involved subjective measures like NASA-TLX and SART, objective task data, and IPA coding from video transcripts. Collaboration mode (HH vs TRH), task difficulty, and cognitive load were key factors.

Results: Robotic assistance reduced perceived workload and improved task fluency in certain conditions. Communication dynamics were more structured and efficient in human-robot teams than human-human pairs.
